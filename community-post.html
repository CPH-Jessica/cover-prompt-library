Got this question from a TIP student this week and I know she is not the only one wondering it:

"Is it okay if I generate the image in AI and then use Book Brush or Canva to build the rest of the cover?"

Babe — yes. That is honestly the smartest workflow you can run right now.

Here is what most people miss.

Generating in AI + finishing in Canva or Book Brush is the path that actually lets you copyright the cover. The US Copyright Office confirmed in February 2025 they WILL register hybrid works where a human did the selecting, arranging, typography, and compositing. That layered design work is yours. Document the prompt, document the design choices, and you have a real copyright claim on the finished piece.

BUT — and read this twice — that workflow does NOT remove the KDP disclosure requirement. If the underlying image came from AI, you still check the "AI-generated" box on KDP. Even after hours of finishing work on top. That is per KDP themselves.

The good news: the disclosure is confidential. It does not show up to readers. It does not hurt your approval. The thing that gets books pulled is failing to disclose, not disclosing.

I am pulling together a full cover design class because honestly, the rules around this are confusing and changing fast — and I would rather you publish with confidence than guess.
